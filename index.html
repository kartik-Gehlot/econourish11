About Us

'ECOSYSTEM' is an innovative web application dedicated to promoting sustainability. It facilitates the collection of food scraps, which are then transformed into high-quality fertilizer, contributing to environmental preservation and waste reduction.

  • Sustainability Focus: 'ECOSYSTEM' is committed to fostering sustainability by reducing waste and promoting eco-friendly practices.
  • Food Scrap Collection: The platform enables the efficient collection of food scraps from users, facilitating a sustainable waste management system.
  • Fertilizer Production: Food scraps are transformed into high-quality fertilizer, contributing to environmental preservation and enriching soil health.

Reporting everyday waste problems to municipalities can often be a complex and time-consuming process. Waste management aims to simplify this by providing an easy-to-use platform. With just a handheld device and internet access, users can quickly report their concerns. The automated system efficiently redirects complaints to the relevant authorities. Municipality admins on the receiving end can acknowledge the reports, allowing users to track the status and know when their concerns are addressed

Guidelines implementation must for proper management of food waste

Disposing of food waste responsibly helps reduce environmental impact. Start by separating food scraps into compostable and non-compostable categories. Compost organic waste like fruit peels, vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and eggshells. Avoid composting meat, dairy, and oily foods, which can attract pests.If composting isn’t an option, consider donating excess edible food to local charities. Use eco-friendly waste disposal services where possible, and reduce food waste by planning meals and using leftovers creatively. Proper disposal conserves resources and reduces landfill burden.

  • 01 “Wastes from health care institutions can be categorized as Organic Food Waste (Compostable) or Non-Organic Food Waste (Non-Compostable)"

    Organic Food Waste (Compostable) consists of fruit/vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, plant-based leftovers, tea bags, and yard trimmings. Non-Organic Food Waste (Non-Compostable) wastes include Plastic packaging, foil wrappers, food-contaminated items, glass containers, synthetic chemicals, processed foods with preservatives, and plastics. reads the guideline.

  • 02 Implementation problem

    The implementation of converting food waste to fertilizer involves collecting organic food scraps, such as vegetable peels and leftovers, and processing them through composting or anaerobic digestion. This process breaks down the waste, turning it into nutrient-rich compost or biogas, which can be used to improve soil health and reduce landfill waste.

  • 03 Dr Rattan Lal, a prominent soil scientist said,

    Rattan Lal emphasizes the importance of recycling organic waste, including food scraps, to restore soil health and combat environmental degradation. He advocates for composting and turning food waste into nutrient-rich fertilizers, which can enhance soil fertility, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and promote sustainable agriculture. According to Lal, converting food waste into fertilizer is a critical step toward building a circular economy and addressing global food security and climate challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Waste management regulations and disposal methods.

  • What is waste management?

    Waste management is the collection, transportation and disposal of waste materials.

  • How often do you collect waste from hostels?

    We typically collect waste three times per week. However, we can adjust the frequency based on your hostel's needs and waste volume.

  • Is there a cost for waste collection?

    Our service includes free collection. Additionally, participating hostels receive discounted fertilizer products made from their waste.

  • How do you ensure the quality of your fertilizer?

    Our products undergo rigorous testing and quality control measures. We monitor nutrient content, maturity, and safety parameters throughout the production process.

  • Can individual students participate in this initiative?

    Yes! We welcome student volunteers and offer educational programs about composting and sustainable waste management.

  • How much food waste is needed to make fertilizer?

    While we accept any amount of food waste, typically 100 kg of food waste produces approximately 30-35 kg of finished compost.
